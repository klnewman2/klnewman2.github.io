Like a Loser

Two Hawks Quarterly Short Story Submission 2024

As much as I wanted them to be, parties are not my thing.

The terrible music blares in my ears and pounds in my heart.

How does anyone like this music? Seriously, the artist isn’t even saying anything! Not anything of meaning anyway.

I scan my surroundings in my safe little corner at the windowsill for the thousandth time. I’m somehow able to hear Kayla’s insanely loud laugh over the music as she’s dancing on Conner. With how she acts in class–all put together and perfect–I have to say it is surprising to watch her grind on him. And by surprising I mean disturbing!

I don’t understand why Kayla would invite me to this party if she was just going to ignore me the whole time. Of course I wasn’t going to say no–I never get invited out to places but, I was hoping she would at least talk to me.

Especially since she is the only friend I have at this party.

And she is my only friend in general.

Everyone here seems to be so carefree and comfortable. It's like they truly like being here–they blend with the music. And I am the exact opposite.

I’m not comfortable, and I am far from carfree. My dress is either too low at the top or too short on the bottom. The music is so ridiculous and obnoxious it’s starting to make my ears bleed. I’m surrounded by horny teenagers who all just want to be nasty. And I can’t even get a drink or a snack without my thoughts screaming at myself to stay in my little corner.

That’s all I’ve ever done–stay where it’s safe. Stay where I’m all alone. And in some cases it’s good, but in most cases it’s just lonely.

I thought coming here would change all of that. I thought…I don’t know…someone would scoop me up and show me around. They’d get to know me and I’d get to know them. They’d ask me to dance and I'd decline but they'd insist and I’d end up doing it and I’d have the time of my life.

But I guess I was stupid to think that.

I was stupid to think that someone would pick me.

I don’t exactly stand out.

I’m not the kind of person that everyone notices. I’m not the kind of person that everyone loves–I’m not even the kind of person people want to be friends with. I’m the kind of person who sits all by herself at a party, dreaming of something that’ll never come true.

It was a mistake coming here. I tried to deny it–but I can’t anymore.

I can’t deny the fact that I belong in my safe little corner.

All alone.

While I’m entranced in my pity party, I hardly notice that Conner is looking at me. Until he starts to stare.

My heartbeat quickens as I wonder why he could possibly be staring at me. Is there something on my face? Is it my face itself?

I try not to look back at him but my efforts are useless–his eyes burning into me make it impossible to look away. He whispers something in Kayla’s ear, and then starts walking over towards me.

Me. 

Why would he walk towards me? Okay, let’s think this through…maybe he’s just going to the kitchen and is taking a weird way.

But he is not just going to the kitchen and taking a weird way.

He is walking straight to me.

He looks me up and down as a smirk spreads across his face.

Oh my god.

“Jada, right?” he asks, incorrectly guessing my name.

“Josephine,” I correct and give a small smile.

“Josephine right. That is what I meant to say,” he says slowly then takes a sip of his drink. He looks me up and down again, and he takes his time with it.

Oh boy.

“You look really good tonight. Like really, really good.”

“You look really, really good too,” I force myself to say.

This is exactly what I wanted–for someone to come up to me. Why aren’t I bouncing off the walls? Granted, he’s not exactly my type, but I should be happy for what I get.

But I’m not.

“Dance with me,” He tells me. Not asks, tells.

“Oh, I don’t know,” I responded, looking down.

“Oh, come on…I promise you won’t regret it,” He pushes, keeping that same smirk on his face as he snatches my hand. His touch sends shivers throughout my whole body.

I take it back. I don’t want any of this, I just want to go home.

I just need to accept that being seen isn’t for me. I am happy being in my safe little corner by myself. Even if it means I’m lonely.

Because it is better than this.

“I should really get going,” I panic and take my hand back. I try to push past him but he stays in front of me like a looming statue.

“The party just started,” He says, a lot less flirtatious and a lot more defensive.

“Yeah, I’m not feeling well so I think I’ll just head home,” I say quickly, trying to move past him again but he still won’t move.

“If I didn’t know any better I would think you were rejecting me,” He whispers in my ear then grabs my arm, “And no one rejects me.”

His grip on my arm is so tight it starts to hurt. I try to clear the lump in my throat but it seems impossible.

I wish I never came to this party.

“I…” I start to say, but I can’t say anything as my mind is flooded with worries.

What is he going to do?

How far will he go?

I don’t feel safe anymore.

“So dance with me,” he orders.

I stand there, speechless. I’m afraid to say no, but there is no way that I am going to say yes. I rack my brain for something to say, but there’s nothing–I’m a deer in headlights.

“CONNER!!” Kayla yells over the music.

Conner and I jump as we look toward her.

“ARE YOU SERIOUSLY FLIRTING WITH ANOTHER GIRL?”

Conner releases me from his grasp and I can breathe again. He storms over to Kayla.

“KAYLA, CALM DOWN, YOU ARE EMBARRASSING ME!”

“I’M EMBARRASSING YOU? YOU’RE EMBARRASSING ME! WE WERE JUST DANCING, I LEAVE YOU ALONE FOR ONE SECOND, AND-”

She yells more but I’m already out of the door. The outside air is refreshing as I feel safe again–taking one breath in and one breath out.

I should’ve known this would happen. I should’ve known that this wouldn’t be anything like I wanted, but much worse. I was stupid to have hope. I was stupid to think that I could have some happy ending.

Because why would a girl like me get something extraordinary like that?

It’s never going to happen, and I just need to accept that. I need to accept that I’m always going to feel alone.

Tears start to well in my eyes as I blink them back–not allowing them to fall until I get into my car.

But apparently I closed my eyes for too long, because the next thing I know, I run into someone and fall back into the grass.

Jesus, Josephine.

“I’m so sorry,” I say, as I get up.

And then I see her.

It’s like time stops and all of the air is pulled out of me.

And the only thing I see is her.

“It’s okay, you’re good,” she chuckles, and it sounds like music to my ears.

None of that horrible music I heard at the party. But soft, romantic music. The kind of song that would get stuck in your head, but you wouldn’t mind it.

I’m still unable to breathe or say a word. I just stand there, with my mouth wide open.

Like an idiot.

She looks at me for a moment, then chuckles again. She stretches her hand out to me, stating, “I’m Noor.”

I take her hand, and the touch sends electricity throughout my whole body. I gaze at her with the same dumb expression on my face. I want to fix it but I can’t.

I’m still in complete awe of how pretty this girl is.

“And you are..?” she asks, helping me out of my trance.

“Josephine! I am–uh my name is Josephine,” I responded.

God please strike me down right now.

She smiles wide again as she laughs, and I look at her in awe again.

She looks back at me, thinking. My heartbeat quickens as I anxiously wait for her to say something.

“You know what? I don’t really feel like partying,” she says. “You?”

I take a deep breath.

Talk and act like a normal person, Josephine.

“Oh, I just got out of there and I am definitely not going back in,” I responded.

“What happened?”

“I just realized that parties are not my thing.”

“And why is that?” she asks, as she walks towards the sidewalk and sits. I walk up to her and sit beside her.

“I’m just really uncomfortable. I don’t like the music, I don’t like the usual attire because this dress is too low and too short, I don’t like to be surrounded by horny teenagers, and then anytime I want to get a drink or a snack I have this little voice in my head that is yelling at me to stay right where I am, which is all by myself.” I admit.

Why did I just spill my entire guts to this girl?

She’s probably thinking I’m some lunatic.

“Wow, that was a lot.” she laughs, showing off her dimples. “But I get it. I mean I feel the exact opposite, but I get it.”

“You enjoy parties?”

“I love them.”

“I don’t get that at all,” I responded, looking up at the stars.

“I mean it’s fun. You get to dance and drink and forget about all of your worries for a while. It’s refreshing and carefree.”

“It is the exact opposite to me. It is extremely stressful and overwhelming.”

“How so? Because of that little voice in your head?”

“Exactly that,” I say, trying to hide the fact that I’m cringing that I told her that.

“Oh, well you just have to get rid of that little voice then,” she states.

She says it so plainly, as if there is an off switch for it.

I look at her with intrigue–Noor has this calmness that exudes off of her. It’s fascinating, because it seems like her mind is peaceful.

I wonder what that’s like.

She looks back at me and I look away so we don’t make eye contact.

“I’m not sure how I can do that,” I say, looking at my feet.

“Well, I just stopped listening to it. I stopped listening to the thoughts that were full of doubts and negativity. And once you stop listening to them, they lose their power, and eventually they go away.” she says, looking straight at me.

God, she’s beautiful. The way she talks, the way she carries herself–it’s all beautiful.

But I’m not supposed to think that.

I keep my eyes to my feet.

“That was–you are–I…like that idea.” I stutter like a loser.

She has every right to walk away from me and call me one.

But she doesn’t. She just smiles at me.

Why would she smile at that? I just made a complete fool of myself for the thousandth time! Maybe she’s making fun of me in her mind but is nice enough to not show it on her face.

“I’m glad you like my ideas.” she continues to smile, and I summon up the courage to smile back at her.

I look into her eyes–they are a dark hazel color, and the prettiest eyes I’ve ever seen.

“I really like your eyes,” she says, causing my jaw to drop.

“My eyes, are you serious? Your eyes are literally majestic.” I say without thinking.

Really, Josephine?

Again, instead of cringing at me, she laughs. “Majestic? Dang, I like that word choice.”

“It’s true,” I say softly, smiling back.
        And we stay like that for a moment, just smiling at each other, and thoughts start to creep in.

Josephine, you aren’t supposed to-

“So tell me more about yourself.” she says, knocking the thoughts out. “Let’s start with the basics. What’s your favorite color?”

“Purple,” I say. “You?”

“Red,” she says, as she turns her head and picks her hair up to show me the back of it, revealing the hidden red in it.

“Hot.” I state without thinking again.

OH MY GOD.

She whips her head back to me with the biggest grin on her face.

“What?”

“I just mean that you’re hot–I MEAN the color red is hot–it looks hot on you–I MEAN…oh my god.” I stumble, until I eventually accept my defeat.

She laughs hard, launching herself backward, and I blush so hard you could literally see red on my chocolate skin.

I literally want to die.

“I am so sorry,” I say, placing my head into my hands.

“Why are you apologizing?” she asks, cheesing.

“Because I am so awkward and weird! I don’t know why you haven’t left me and called me a loser.”

“Because I don’t think you’re any of those things,” she says, causing me to look at her to see if she’s being serious.

Because how could she not think those things after everything I’ve done and said for the past five minutes?

“I think you’re cute and honest. And I really like that.” she smiles wide, staring deep into my eyes.

I can’t help but to smile back, as my heart pounds in my head.

I can’t believe someone as amazing as her thinks that way about me.

Me.

Josephine, this isn’t right.

“I’m glad and surprised you think that way about me,” I smile, pushing the intruding thought out of my head.

“Why surprised?” she asked me.

“Well, no one has ever really called me cute before,” I state, looking at the ground.

“No, I don’t believe that.”
        “It’s true–why would I lie about that?”

“You’re literally stunning, Josephine.”

I jolted my head to look at her again.

No way she actually thinks that.

But there is no sign of lying on her face. She thinks I’m stunning.

Her.

I just look at her in disbelief, and she just smiles back at me.

“Okay, favorite band?” she asks.

“Paramore,” I state.

“Basic.”

“What? They are not basic–they are amazing.”

“Oh I didn’t say they weren’t, I just said they’re basic.”
        “Okay fine, what’s yours?”

“Lovejoy.”

“I have never heard of that band.”

“Oh you gotta listen to it,” she says as her eyes light up. “Promise me you’ll listen to it when you get home.”

“Okay I guess I will give them a shot since they’re your favorite,” I smile.

“Good. Alright, what are your hobbies?” she asks.

“I really like to write.”

“Ooh, what do you write?”

“Stories, poems, that kind of stuff.”

“Same! Well, I write songs, but songs are basically the same as poems.”

I laugh as I say, “Yea, they are basically the same.”

“So why do you like writing?” she asks, cocking her head.

“Uhm…well first you can do it all alone so that’s nice,” I start and she laughs. “But I really like creating a new world. It kind of allows you to travel to a new world yourself, and forget about your own for a while. I like writing about romances I wish I had or powers that would be really cool, or a whole new universe that would be mesmerizing. And I like writing stories that are happy to make people smile, and stories that are sad to make people cry, and stories where the character wants something so bad, but they just don’t get it. I like writing about the human experience–and feelings that are just like everyone else’s, so the reader knows that they aren’t alone. That what they are feeling is what a lot of other people are feeling too. From a teenager in high school to an all-powerful God. Because what they’re feeling is real, normal, and okay.” I say, looking to the stars.

A moment passes and then I look at her, waiting for her to say something.

“You’re really beautiful,” she states, cheesing.

Fireworks go off in my chest.

“You’re really beautiful too,” I smile at her, but then I correct myself. My heart pounds out of my chest and bangs in my head.

I’m not supposed to feel this way.

I jump and say, “I’m sorry, I can’t do this.”

I’m walking away from her, hoping she won’t follow me but hoping she does at the same time.

And she does.

“Wait, what?” She calls after me.

“I can’t–I’m not supposed to–”

“If I made you uncomfortable I’m sorry but–”

I stop and turn to her.

“No you didn’t. You didn’t at all, it's just–it’s not you–at all. It’s me.”

My breathing quickens as thoughts flood into my head.

What would your parents think about this?

What about your classmates?

The whole school?

They’d be disgusted.

This isn’t right, Josephine.

You aren’t supposed to feel this way about a girl, Josephine.
        You are disgusting.

The world starts to spin and all of the air is sucked from out of me.

“Josephine? Are you okay?” Noor asks, but I can barely hear her.

“What’s happening to me?” I whisper.

“Josephine? Listen to me, okay? You need to breathe,” she says, as she takes my hands into hers. “Breathe in, breathe out, breathe in, breathe out.”

“Everything is spinning.”

“You have to calm down, Josephine. Listen, you are in control, everything is going to be okay. You have to breathe. Look at me. Breathe in…breathe out…breathe in…breathe out…”

She does that until the world stops spinning. My heartbeat slows and I can hear her clearly.

“What was that?” I ask.

That has never happened to me before. I get overwhelmed all of the time, but never like that.

That felt like I was going to die.

“I think you had a panic attack,” she tells me. “Do you want to go inside to get some water?”

I can’t believe I just had a complete breakdown in front of this girl.

A beautiful girl, at that.

“I think it’s time for me to go home,” I say, praying that this is just a nightmare.

“You don’t have to be embarrassed, Josephine,” she says, looking in my eyes. I look back at her for a moment–she’s completely genuine.

“Why haven’t you walked away from me yet?” I ask her.

She smiles at me, and says, “Because if it wasn’t obvious–I like you. I like spending time with you. So don’t go just yet?”

I really want to say yes.

I really want to stay with this amazing girl that somehow thinks I’m amazing too.

But I can’t.

“I’m sorry,” I say, as I turn away from her. “My parents would kill me if they knew what I was doing right now…if they knew how I was feeling about you.”

“They don’t know that you’re…?” Noor starts.

“I don’t even know that I am,” I say, turning to her. “But considering how I’ve acted tonight and how I feel about you, I guess I should be more sure of it.”

“Uhh yeah, you definitely should!” She exclaims, and I can’t help but let out a small chuckle.

“Listen,” she says, as she walks closer to me, and my heartbeat grows faster with each step she takes. “I can’t tell you how to live your life, but I can tell you that it is miserable trying to be something you’re not. And who cares what your parents say, or your family, or your friends, or whoever! If they don’t support you, then they never supported you in the first place. Forget them!”

“That’s a lot easier said than done, Noor.”

“I know. But I’m not asking you to tell everyone here that you like girls–I’m asking you to stay with me. To feel whatever you feel, and to live in the moment.” she says, as she takes my hands, and the touch sends electricity throughout my entire body.

I finally know what it feels like.

I finally know what it feels like to be wanted. And not alone. And that’s all I’ve ever wanted. Noor has given me everything I have dreamed and hoped for.

The least I could do is say yes.

“Well, when you put it like that it’s impossible to say no,” I say, as I give her a small smile.

“Yea that’s what I was going for,” she smiles wide.

I wish that could be it. I wish I could just relax and enjoy this beautiful girl in front of me.

But my mind won’t let me do that.

My mind won’t let me enjoy the fact that I have everything I’ve ever wanted, because I’m afraid of what everyone would think. I’m afraid that because I like girls, my family won’t love me anymore.

And that’s terrifying.

It’s terrifying that one detail about you can make someone you love, not love you back.

“You know what I think could help you live in the moment?” Noor asks, as if she can hear all of my worries.

“What?” I ask, happy that she is knocking me out of my spiral.

“Dancing with me,” she smiles, as she stretches her hand out to me.

“Oh, you want me to go back there? I don’t know…”

“Come on…I promise you won’t regret it.”

She raises her eyes at me and smiles even wider. Her inviting eyes make it really hard to say no. Even though I really want to.

“Hmmm…one song.”

“Yes!”

“But if I’m still not having fun will we come back out here?”

“That’s not going to happen but…sure,” she smirks at me.

I take her hand and she walks me towards the house. My heartbeat pounds louder as we get closer and closer to the front door. We get right in front of the door, and somehow my heartbeat is louder than the blaring music.

But then, Noor looks back at me and smiles. And suddenly, the beating calms down. Instead of my heart feeling tight, it feels light.

I smile back at her as we walk inside. I pray I don’t see Conner or Kayla, not knowing who would give me the most trouble.

A not-so-terrible pop song starts playing, and Noor pulls me closer. She smiles at me and I smile back as we rock back and forth to the beat of the music. She takes my hand, and twirls me around, and my smile grows even wider.

And for the first time in a long time, I feel free.

I don’t care if other people are looking.

For right now, I don’t care what people will think when they know.

I’m not even scared about what will happen tomorrow, and everyday after that.

All that matters is right here, and right now.

I have her.

I’m not alone anymore.

And it’s the best feeling ever.
